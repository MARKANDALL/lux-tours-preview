# Landing Page Teardown Framework

## The first screen (before any scroll)

- What does the headline say — promise, description, or provocation?
- How long is it — three words or fifteen?
- What's the visual doing — video, still, product shot, illustration, nothing?
- How much breathing room around it all?
- Could you tell what Chrome is from this screen alone?

## The opening move (right after the hero)

- What's the first thing they show — features, story, social proof, demo?
- What does that choice say about what they think matters most?

## Section rhythm

- Are sections evenly spaced, or do some sprawl and some snap by?
- Where do they slow down and let you breathe?
- Where do they pack tight?
- Rough screen-heights per section?

## Headline craft within sections

- Do titles tell you what's there, or tease?
- Sentences, fragments, or single words?
- Same length across the page, or varied?
- Active verbs or noun phrases?

## Body copy length

- How many words do they spend explaining something — one sentence, paragraph, two?
- Where do they trust the visual to do the work?
- Where do they trust the words?

## Reading level

- Could a non-native English speaker get through it?
- Are there technical terms — and do they explain or assume?

## Visual hierarchy

- What does your eye hit first, second, third on each section?
- Is that order on purpose?
- One clear star per section, or competing elements?

## Color discipline

- Where does the brand color show up?
- Where does it hold back?
- How many colors on the page total?
- Lots of white, or is the background doing work?

## Imagery and motion

- Static or moving?
- Real screenshots, illustrations, or lifestyle photography?
- Ambient motion (looping) or triggered (scroll-activated)?
- How often does motion happen — every section, or just a few?

## Negative space

- How much air around the headline?
- Around buttons?
- Between sections?

## CTAs (the buttons)

- What do they actually say — exact words?
- Where are they placed — top, bottom, both?
- How many buttons per page total?
- All going to the same place, or different?

## Proof and evidence

- Testimonials, stats, press logos, awards, screenshots?
- How do they prove what they're claiming?
- Where on the page is the proof?

## Transitions between sections

- How do you know one section ended and another began — color change, divider, white space, bleed?

## Voice and tone

- Personality of the writing — confident, playful, reverent, geeky, warm, efficient?
- Same voice across the whole page, or does it shift?

## What's NOT there

- What would a worse marketing site put here that Chrome left off?
- Popups, exit-intent modals, newsletter intrusions, huge cookie banners, long testimonial sections?
- Note the omissions — they're decisions.

## The closing move

- How does the page end — CTA, quiet link, designed footer, tacked-on footer?
- Does it feel like a landing, or a fade-out?

## Navigation

- Top nav — how many items?
- Sticky on scroll? Does it change?
- Footer — minimal or sprawling?

## The "huh" moments

- Anywhere you scrolled and thought *wait, what just happened* or *why did they do it that way*?
- Surprise is signal — write those down.

## The pace overall

- Did you feel like you went on a journey, or read a brochure?
- Did you get tired anywhere?
- Did you skim anywhere?
- Where did your attention break?
